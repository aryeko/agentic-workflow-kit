# Design → plan (supporting product)

Converts a design into a package-ready **execution plan** in the expected schema, applying
the author's planning experience. An optional accelerator: bring-your-own plan is supported
as long as it meets the schema. The kit enables and supports here — it does not limit.

## Sub-modules (high level)

The execution-plan schema (the package's input contract) · decomposition into stories /
dependency order · acceptance criteria + evidence clauses · guidelines & best-practices doc.

## Known from this session — address when expanding

- **Produces output conformant to the execution-plan schema** — Jig owns that schema;
  design→plan produces what `jig` consumes, conformant to it. Coordinate with
  [jig.md](jig.md); the schema is the seam between these two products.
- **Prior art:** v-next `plan-epic` + `plan-delivery` (story DAG, story contracts,
  falsifiable ACs, evidence clauses, predicate-input sourcing); v0.7.0 `plan-delivery-track`.
- **Fold in the lessons:** prose-only safeguards reproduce defects — gates must be checkable
  boxes (`docs/implementation-authoring/` + the lessons ledger); watch the predicate-input /
  closure defect classes.
- **Optional** because the author doesn't claim to be the best planner — strong default,
  override welcome.
- Likely the **second** deep-dive after the package, since it produces the package's input.
